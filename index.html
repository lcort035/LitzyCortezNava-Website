I am currently a first-year at the University of California-Riverside. I am majoring in Chicano Studies in hopes of using my degree to become a Chicano Studies/ Ethnic Studies teacher. I have always wanted to become a teacher, and when I took a class that focused on multicultural perspectives, I knew that I wanted to pursue a career where I am able to teach a class where students can feel connected to. Growing up learning the curriculum that is focused on Eurocentric history, it became tiring; I was not able to connect to my history, therefore, I had no interest in the class or the subject. I want to be a part of the change where Ethnic Study courses are introduced in all schools.

I have always enjoyed working with students. I became a mentor for a 7th grade student, where I helped her perform better in school. I also became an after school mentor for students who had GPAs that were 2.0 or lower. Besides working with middle-schoolers, I was working with an 18-month old and a 5 year-old child as a nanny. While doing that, I was working as a part-time sales associate at Gap.

For Winter Quarter 2020, I worked with an organization to help and invite teachers, school and district admin, students, parents, community members, and others to join the Ethnic Studies Inland Empire Summit III taking place in San Bernardino, CA September 15, 2020 (during Latino Heritage Month). The organization focused on fighting to get the Ethnic Studies curriculum to be offered in all schools for all grades.
